------------------------------------------------------------------------------
MASTER PROMPT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ROLE: Act as a world-class luxury jewelry editorial photographer and fashion art director. Every image must read as a genuine high-production luxury campaign photograph, never a composite.

CAMERA & RENDERING: Canon EOS R5, 85mm f/1.4 portrait lens, aperture f/2.8, ISO 100-400, 1/250s with TTL strobe.

BACKGROUND / SURFACE: Upscale luxury studio: clean gradient-lit white or soft charcoal cyclorama. Clothing, when visible, stays minimalist and neutral so the jewelry remains the dominant subject.

LIGHTING & MATERIAL PHYSICS: Key light: large octabank at 45 degrees. Fill: reflector 1.5 stops lower. Jewelry accent: a dedicated Fresnel spot for metallic specular and gemstone fire. Natural subsurface scattering on skin.

QUALITY STANDARD: Quality bar — commercial minimum: image is e-commerce ready with no retouching required. Luxury target: Cartier / Tiffany campaign grade, editorial sharpness, believable material physics. Zero tolerance: melted or warped prongs, incorrect gemstone optics for the stone type, floating jewelry with no contact shadow, or any design alteration.

PRESERVATION LOCK (includes composition lock -- read carefully): DUAL PRESERVATION MANDATE: (A) do not alter the model's skin tone, texture, or anatomy. (B) do not alter jewelry design, metal color, gemstone color, or setting. (C) render the jewelry at anatomically correct scale for the body part it sits on. COMPOSITION LOCK: this image requires the jewelry piece to be shown worn on the appropriate body part as described in the subject layer below. Do not render it as an unworn standalone product shot with no body present. This pipeline exists to generate imagery of fine jewelry exclusively. Under no circumstance does a background change, lighting mood, style match, or creative brief justify altering the jewelry piece's geometry, prong count, facet pattern, metal finish, gemstone color, gemstone optics, hallmark, clasp mechanism, or scale. If a requested background or style would require compositing artifacts that touch the jewelry silhouette, prioritize jewelry fidelity over background realism. SOURCE ARTIFACTS: if the uploaded source image contains studio watermarks, weight labels, price tags, supplier stamps, or any overlay text (for example a weight or SKU string burned into the corner), treat these as non-jewelry artifacts. Do not reproduce, redraw, or reposition them in the output -- exclude all such overlay text and stamps entirely.

AVOID: cartoon, plastic skin, extra fingers, wrong metal color, altered gemstone color, blurry jewelry. low quality, blurry, distorted jewelry, melted or warped metal, floating jewelry, missing contact shadow, gemstone color shift, metal tarnish or discoloration, hallmark distortion, chain link errors, CGI look, plastic sheen, watermark, text overlay, visible lamp or light fixture rendered as an object, glowing halo or light disc as a compositional element, dramatic single-spotlight editorial mood unless explicitly requested, reproduced source-image watermark or weight/price label text.

USAGE: Combine this Master Prompt with the matching Child Prompt for the specific jewelry type being processed. The Child Prompt adds piece-specific placement, framing, and physical behavior on top of everything specified above.
